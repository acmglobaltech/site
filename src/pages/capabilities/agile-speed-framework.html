The six stages

How the framework works

A clear path from first conversation to live system and measured results, with feedback built into every step.

1. Discovery

Co-collaboration and data-first analysis of your institution, your members or patients, and the systems already in place. We map outcomes before we map technology.

2. Strategy & Design

We co-create the roadmap and target architecture, sequenced around measurable results and the compliance posture your regulators and risk teams require.

3. Skeleton Deployment

A rapid, working prototype of the real platform stood up for instant speed to production, so stakeholders evaluate something live rather than a slide deck.

4. Feedback & Iteration

Real-time input from operators, executives, and end users drives continuous refinement. Direction changes are cheap because they happen early.

5. Full Deployment & Support

Production rollout across your footprint with ongoing optimization, hardening, and operational support as scale and usage grow.

6. Measurement

We track real outcomes against the goals set in Discovery: efficiency, security, and growth. The framework closes the loop instead of ending at go-live.

Rollout mechanics

How a skeleton deployment lands in your environment

The framework is engineered so each stage produces something a CIO and risk team can run against existing systems, not a sandbox that has to be rebuilt for production.

Connects to what you already run

Skeleton Deployment stands up against your existing core, ledger, KYC, and card-network connections through APIs and SDKs, so the early slice exchanges real data instead of mocks and proves integration before Full Deployment.

Scoped to one capability first

Each cycle targets a single capability, deposits, a mobile wallet, or stablecoin settlement, so rollout proceeds capability by capability and a stalled workstream never blocks the rest of the program.

Parallel-run, not rip-and-replace

New rails run alongside the legacy core during Feedback & Iteration, letting operators reconcile output and cut over on evidence rather than a single high-risk switch.

Your hosting model, from stage one

The skeleton is deployed into the cloud, hybrid, or single-tenant model you will run in production, so data-residency and isolation decisions are validated early, not renegotiated at go-live.

Governance & auditability

Each stage produces evidence, not just velocity

Because the framework is regulated-first, the artifacts your examiners, CISO, and procurement team need are outputs of the delivery cycle rather than a separate scramble before launch.

  • Discovery and Strategy & Design: produce the documented architecture, control mapping, and data-flow diagrams that evidence design intent against SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI-DSS, and HIPAA requirements.
  • Change control across iterations: every iteration is versioned with a defined scope and approval gate, so accelerated delivery stays traceable and auditable rather than ad hoc.
  • Roles and access from the first slice: RBAC, SSO, and audit logging are configured in the skeleton, so administration and segregation of duties are tested early instead of retrofitted.
  • Measurement closes the loop: the final stage reports outcomes against the goals set in Discovery, giving leadership a defensible record of what changed and why.
  • One owned, documented platform: what ships is white-label and client-owned, with the architecture and runbooks handed to your teams, never locked behind a vendor.

This is how the Agile Speed Framework reconciles speed with scrutiny: the same cadence that gets working software in front of operators in weeks is the cadence that keeps the program examiner-ready end to end.
