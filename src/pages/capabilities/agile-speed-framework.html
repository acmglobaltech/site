Why it exists

Legacy delivery timelines are the real risk

Most core and platform programs stall because change is treated as a multi-year event instead of a continuous practice. The Agile Speed Framework is built to remove that drag.

Financial institutions do not lose ground because they lack ambition. They lose ground because every change to a legacy core is slow, expensive, and risky, so necessary work gets deferred. The Agile Speed Framework is ACM Global Tech's answer: a structured, repeatable way to design, ship, and prove banking technology in short cycles, without trading away the rigor a regulated institution requires.

The principle is simple. We deliver a working slice of the real system early, put it in front of the people who will run it, and iterate against measurable outcomes. You see progress in weeks, give feedback against something tangible, and keep control of scope, brand, and architecture the entire way.

The six stages

How the framework works

A clear path from first conversation to live system and measured results, with feedback built into every step.

1. Discovery

Co-collaboration and data-first analysis of your institution, your members or patients, and the systems already in place. We map outcomes before we map technology.

2. Strategy & Design

We co-create the roadmap and target architecture, sequenced around measurable results and the compliance posture your regulators and risk teams require.

3. Skeleton Deployment

A rapid, working prototype of the real platform stood up for instant speed to production, so stakeholders evaluate something live rather than a slide deck.

4. Feedback & Iteration

Real-time input from operators, executives, and end users drives continuous refinement. Direction changes are cheap because they happen early.

5. Full Deployment & Support

Production rollout across your footprint with ongoing optimization, hardening, and operational support as scale and usage grow.

6. Measurement

We track real outcomes against the goals set in Discovery: efficiency, security, and growth. The framework closes the loop instead of ending at go-live.

What makes it different

Speed that holds up to scrutiny

Fast delivery is only useful if the result is something a bank or credit union can actually run, defend, and own.

  • Skeleton deployments: we lead with a live, functional slice of the production system, so speed to production is real, not a prototype thrown away later.
  • Regulated-first by design: compliance, auditability, and controls are part of the build from stage one, not retrofitted before launch. The work is engineered to be examiner-ready and defensible.
  • Post-quantum security from day one: every solution is built with post-quantum cryptography, protecting long-lived financial records against harvest-now-decrypt-later exposure.
  • Client-owned and white-label: you brand and own what we deliver. The framework keeps architecture and intellectual property in your hands, never locked behind a vendor.
  • Flexible delivery models: onshore, nearshore, and offshore teams are matched to your timeline, budget, and risk tolerance.
  • Built for cost efficiency: the platform targets up to 95% lower infrastructure cost versus legacy core stacks, and the framework is how that value gets delivered without a disruptive rip-and-replace.
