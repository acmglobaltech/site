The deadline just moved to 2030

A new White House executive order, Securing the Nation against Advanced Cryptographic Attacks, requires high-value and high-impact systems to adopt post-quantum key establishment by December 31, 2030 and quantum-safe signatures by December 31, 2031, years sooner than the prior 2035 timeline. For regulated finance, crypto-agility just became a compliance deadline. ACM is built to meet it today.

The Mandate

What the order requires

The threat moved: recent research sharply lowered the resources needed to break RSA and elliptic-curve cryptography, and Google and Cloudflare pulled their own timelines forward to 2029. The order responds by compressing the federal transition.

  • Key establishment by Dec 31, 2030: post-quantum schemes (ML-KEM / FIPS 203) for establishing shared secrets on high-value and high-impact systems.
  • Signatures by Dec 31, 2031: quantum-safe digital signatures (ML-DSA / FIPS 204, SLH-DSA / FIPS 205).
  • A cryptographic bill of materials (CBOM): NIST and CISA will issue guidance on inventorying every cryptographic component, library, and module, you cannot migrate what you cannot see.
  • Covered contractors: procurement rules extend the same readiness deadlines and vulnerability-disclosure expectations to vendors.
  • Coordination: a government-wide process led by OMB and the National Cyber Director, with foreign-government engagement to adopt NIST-standardized PQC.
The ACM Approach

At the 2030/2031 target today, not racing toward it

ACM's regulated stack treats post-quantum security as the default, built in from genesis rather than retrofitted before a deadline. That is the difference between a migration project and a platform you can already stand on.

  • NIST algorithms as native precompiles: ML-KEM, ML-DSA, and SLH-DSA run on the Lux post-quantum ledger at genesis, so key establishment and signatures are already quantum-safe.
  • Hybrid, non-disruptive migration: classical and post-quantum signatures verify side by side, so existing workflows keep running while you transition, no flag day.
  • Crypto-agility as a control: primitives are abstracted from the application layer, so algorithms rotate as standards evolve, exactly what the order asks institutions to build.
  • CBOM & discovery: ACM can help inventory quantum-vulnerable cryptography across your stack and prioritize it by data secrecy-lifetime and exposure, the harvest-now-decrypt-later risk to long-lived financial records.
  • Threshold & non-custodial: MPC threshold custody so no single key or party controls settlement, hardening high-value operations beyond the algorithm swap.
