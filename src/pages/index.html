Post-quantum

The hardest problem in security, solved at the frontier

Quantum computers will break the public-key cryptography behind nearly every system online. Adversaries already harvest encrypted data today to decrypt it the moment they can. NIST has finalized the standards, and the migration is no longer optional. It is not just banks: every institution running critical infrastructure has to move, and the deepest parts of that move are the hardest. That is exactly where we work.

Find what's exposed

We inventory the quantum-vulnerable cryptography across your stack, from the core to the edge, so you know precisely what has to change.

Migrate without the rip-out

Crypto-agile migration to NIST post-quantum algorithms, layered into your existing infrastructure with hybrid classical-plus-PQ schemes.

Solve the threshold problem

Most vendors stop at swapping in a NIST default. We build post-quantum threshold cryptography: distributed signing with no single point of compromise. This is the frontier, and the hardest part.

Real cryptography, submitted to NIST. Not slideware.

Our post-quantum threshold work is open source and standards-aligned: an active submission to NIST's Multi-Party Threshold Cryptography process, grounded in peer-reviewed research.
