The context

The share register fell behind.

The transfer agent holds the official record of who owns what, yet most registers still live in batch-bound, siloed systems that drift from every other copy of the truth. As securities tokenize, the register and the on-chain asset must stay in lockstep, and the cryptography protecting decades-long ownership records has to outlast the quantum threat.

Silos

Fractured records

The register, the cap table, and the issuer's books each keep a copy. Reconciliation is constant, costly, and error-prone.

Manual

Corporate actions

Dividends, splits, and voting are run by hand across spreadsheets, where a single mistake ripples through every holder.

Tokenization

Two sources of truth

A tokenized security and its register can diverge. Ownership of record has to reconcile to the on-chain asset, continuously.

RSA / ECC

Quantum-vulnerable

Records meant to last decades are secured by cryptography a quantum computer will break. The exposure is live now.
