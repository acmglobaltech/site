The challenge

Treasury runs on yesterday's data and tomorrow's deadlines

Most treasury functions still stitch together bank portals, spreadsheets, and batch files. Cash position is a day late, FX is opaque, and cross-border payments settle on schedules set by correspondent banks rather than by the business.

For corporate treasurers, finance leaders at community and mid-sized banks, and treasury operations inside healthcare organizations, that lag is not a back-office inconvenience — it is working capital trapped in transit, hedging done on stale numbers, and counterparty risk that nobody can see until reconciliation. ACM exists to close that gap with infrastructure designed for regulated institutions from the first line of code.

Administration & audit

Governance and auditability built for treasury controls

Treasury moves real money, so approvals, entitlements, and an evidentiary record are enforced in the platform rather than left to spreadsheets and email.

  • Role-based entitlements: scope who can view positions, initiate payments, set counterparty limits, or release FX trades by entity, currency, and amount.
  • Maker-checker workflows: segregation of duties and configurable approval thresholds on every payment, sweep, and tokenized settlement.
  • SSO & identity: integrate with your SAML or OIDC identity provider so treasury access follows your existing provisioning and de-provisioning.
  • Immutable audit trails: timestamped logs of instructions, approvals, rate captures, and limit changes — exportable for internal audit and regulator review.
  • Post-quantum protection: payment instructions and balance data are secured by default, supporting SOC 2, ISO 27001, and PCI-DSS control requirements.
