The challenge

Legacy rails were not built for 24/7 markets

Broker-dealers operate where capital markets, payments, and compliance intersect. Most are doing it on infrastructure that batches, settles slowly, and stops at the border.

Cross-border execution still depends on correspondent chains and cut-off windows. Multi-currency books carry FX spread and reconciliation drag. Client cash sits idle between trade and settlement. And as markets compress toward shorter settlement cycles and continuous trading, the operational gap between the front office and the back office keeps widening.

ACM consolidates the plumbing. One banking core handles deposits and client money, payments and settlement, multi-currency FX, treasury and liquidity, and tokenized real-world assets, all under your brand and your control.

Controls & auditability

Built for the books-and-records your examiners expect

Broker-dealers answer to FINRA and the SEC on customer-asset protection, recordkeeping, and supervision. ACM is designed to support SOC 2, ISO 27001, and PCI-DSS requirements and to give risk and compliance the evidence they need.

  • Segregation of client assets: firm and customer cash, positions, and tokenized holdings are tracked on separate ledgers with configurable custody, supporting customer-protection obligations such as Rule 15c3-3.
  • Immutable books and records: every settlement, FX fill, and asset transfer writes a tamper-evident, time-stamped audit log designed to support 17a-3 and 17a-4 recordkeeping and retention.
  • RBAC, SSO, and approvals: role-based entitlements, single sign-on via your identity provider, and maker-checker approvals enforce supervision and segregation of duties across desks and operations.
  • Surveillance-ready data: a single, queryable record feeds your trade-surveillance and reporting tools so reconstruction and exception review run on current, unified data.
  • Post-quantum protection: keys, custody, and settlement messaging are secured with post-quantum cryptography as a default, not an add-on.
