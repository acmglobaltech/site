Overview

A complete card program, white-label from day one

Card issuing is no longer a back-office utility — it is how members and customers experience your institution every day. ACM gives you the full issuing lifecycle in one place: design, provisioning, authorization, spend controls, and settlement, all brandable and client-owned.

Because issuing sits inside the broader ACM banking ecosystem — core banking, deposits and lending, payments, treasury, and FX — your cards share one ledger and one risk view. There is no reconciliation gap between the card program and the account it draws from, and no separate processor relationship to manage on the side.

The result is a card platform that behaves like part of your bank, because it is. You set the brand, the rules, and the economics. ACM provides the rails, the security, and the speed to production.

Built for Regulated Institutions

Compliance-ready, secured for the long term

Card data outlives most technology decisions. ACM is engineered to keep it protected and examiner-ready across the life of the program.

  • Post-quantum cryptography: sensitive card and account data is protected against "harvest now, decrypt later" attacks, not just today's threats.
  • Regulated-first by design: built for the controls, audit trails, and reporting that bank and credit-union examiners expect.
  • One ledger, one risk view: issuing shares the ACM core, so fraud signals, balances, and limits stay consistent in real time.
  • Client-owned and brandable: the program, the data, and the customer relationship stay with your institution.
  • Lower total cost: ACM targets up to 95% lower infrastructure cost versus legacy core stacks as a platform value proposition.

Rollout & Administration

Launch a program in phases, with full control and audit

ACM's Agile Speed Framework brings a card program to production in defined stages, so risk and operations teams keep oversight at every step rather than approving one large cutover.

  • Phased launch path: pilot virtual cards to a small cardholder cohort, validate controls and dispute handling, then scale to physical issuing and your full portfolio.
  • Role-based program administration: RBAC and SSO govern who can issue cards, change spend rules, approve disputes, and view PAN-level data, separating program, fraud, and support duties.
  • Auditable by design: every card action, limit change, and authorization decision is logged with immutable trails examiners and your internal audit can review.
  • Enterprise support & SLAs: defined response targets for authorization issues and incidents, with a named team across pilot, launch, and steady-state operation.
