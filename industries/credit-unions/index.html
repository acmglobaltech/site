The Challenge

The big-bank gap is a technology gap

Members compare your mobile app to the largest banks in the country, then decide whether to keep their direct deposit with you.

Credit unions win on trust, service, and rates. They lose ground when the digital experience lags, when a legacy core makes every change slow and costly, and when new payment rails arrive faster than a vendor roadmap can absorb them.

ACM closes that gap without asking a credit union to become a technology company. The platform is brandable end to end, so the relationship stays between the member and the institution, never the vendor. And because it targets up to 95% lower infrastructure cost versus legacy core stacks, modernizing does not have to mean a budget your board cannot defend.

Why ACM

Built for institutions examiners scrutinize

Credit unions operate under real oversight. The platform is engineered for that reality from day one.

  • Post-quantum cryptography: forward-looking encryption that protects member data against tomorrow's threats, not just today's.
  • Client-owned and brandable: the entire ecosystem is white-label, keeping the member relationship and the data with the credit union.
  • Agile Speed Framework: a delivery model built to ship production-ready capabilities fast, so your roadmap keeps pace with member expectations.

Integration & Interoperability

Connects to the core and CUSOs you already run

Most credit unions cannot rip out a core on a board's timeline, and they should not have to. ACM is built to sit alongside what you operate today and take over function by function.

Core & processor connectors

REST APIs and pre-built connectors interoperate with incumbent cores and third-party processors, so deposits, loans, and member records stay in sync while you modernize in stages rather than in one cutover.

Card networks & rails

SDKs for debit and credit issuing, plus connectivity to ACH, wire, instant-payment, and shared-branch networks, so a card or rail goes live without re-platforming the whole stack.

BSA/AML & KYC hooks

Integration points for your existing member onboarding, OFAC screening, and BSA monitoring tools keep current compliance workflows intact instead of forcing a parallel system.

Examiner-ready data export

Structured data and report feeds for 5300 Call Report preparation, share-insurance reconciliation, and NCUA examination requests, exportable on demand rather than rebuilt by hand.

Because the ecosystem is client-owned and API-first, your team configures these connections rather than filing a vendor ticket and waiting in a queue.

Administration & Auditability

Control sized for a lean credit union and a real exam

A small operations team has to administer the platform, and an NCUA examiner has to be able to trace what it did. Both are designed in, not bolted on.

  • Role-based access: granular RBAC for tellers, lenders, back-office, and board-reporting roles, with segregation of duties enforced so dual-control and maker-checker steps match credit-union policy.
  • SSO and member-data protection: single sign-on for staff against your identity provider, with post-quantum cryptography protecting member PII and account data end to end.
  • Immutable audit logs: every configuration change, approval, and member-record access is logged and retained, giving examiners and your supervisory committee a defensible trail.
  • Compliance-ready by design: architecture designed to support SOC 2, ISO 27001, and PCI-DSS requirements, so vendor due-diligence and exam prep draw on the same controls.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Is ACM's platform white-label for credit unions?

Yes. The entire ecosystem, from core banking and card issuing to mobile wallets and FX, is client-owned and brandable. Members interact with your credit union's brand, and the data and member relationship stay with the institution, not the vendor.

How does ACM support compliance and examiners?

The platform is built regulated-first, with compliance-ready architecture and post-quantum cryptography designed for institutions under active oversight. We position it for regulated institutions rather than retrofitting controls after the fact.

Can a smaller credit union afford to modernize with ACM?

ACM targets up to 95% lower infrastructure cost versus legacy core stacks as a core value proposition, and the Agile Speed Framework is built for fast, production-ready delivery, so modernization fits budgets and timelines a board can defend.

Do we have to adopt tokenization and stablecoins right away?

No. Real-world asset tokenization, stablecoin settlement, and multi-currency FX are part of the same platform but available as optional, often non-custodial capabilities you can adopt when your members and regulators are ready.
