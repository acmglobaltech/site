Built-in DEX

Trade and settle, on-chain and atomic.

A built-in decentralized exchange with both order-book and AMM liquidity, where every trade settles atomically on-chain. No external venue, no settlement risk, and compliant trading rails wired into the platform.

The problem

Settlement is where trades go wrong.

Bolting an external exchange onto a regulated platform inherits its worst properties: trades that clear off-venue and settle later, custody handed to a third party, and thin liquidity that moves prices against your users. The gap between trade and settlement is where risk lives.

Settlement risk

The gap before final

When a trade and its settlement are separate steps, a counterparty can fail in between, leaving one side exposed.

Custody

Assets off your platform

Routing to an external venue means surrendering custody mid-trade, adding counterparty exposure you do not control.

Liquidity

One source is never enough

Order books and AMMs each thin out in different conditions; relying on a single model leaves users with worse fills.

Compliance

Trading off the rails

External venues sit outside your controls, making it hard to enforce the policy and reporting a regulated market demands.

What we deliver

Exchange and settlement, in one venue.

Not a bridge to someone else's exchange: a native trading engine where matching and settlement are the same atomic on-chain event.

Order-book matching

A native limit order book for price-time priority and the tight spreads professional flow expects.

AMM liquidity

Automated market-maker pools provide continuous liquidity, so there is always a price even in thin markets.

Atomic on-chain settlement

Match and settlement are one indivisible transaction: the trade either completes in full or not at all, with no settlement gap. This is the part that removes the risk.

Non-custodial trading

Assets settle directly between parties on-chain, so no third party ever takes custody mid-trade.

Compliant rails

Policy and reporting controls are wired into the venue, so trading stays inside your regulatory perimeter.

Built-in, not bolted-on

The exchange is part of the platform, sharing identity, custody, and settlement rather than integrating across a boundary.

The proof

Open source. On-chain. Verifiable settlement.

A real exchange you can read and verify, not slideware.

Atomic by construction

Settlement is part of the matching transaction, so there is no window in which a trade is agreed but not final.

Hybrid liquidity

Order-book and AMM liquidity coexist in one venue, giving users the better price across both at all times.

Open source

The exchange and settlement logic live in public repositories, so how trades clear is auditable, not opaque.
