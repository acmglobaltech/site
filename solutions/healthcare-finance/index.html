The problem

Administrative waste is the silent line item

A large share of US healthcare spend never touches patient care. It is consumed by claims rework, manual reconciliation, slow remittance, and the cost of moving money between providers, payers, and patients.

ACM treats payment and revenue-cycle infrastructure as a financial system, not a clearinghouse afterthought. We bring core banking, settlement, and treasury capabilities directly into the healthcare flow so finance teams can shorten cycles, reduce exception handling, and free up working capital that is otherwise trapped in receivables.

Delivery

Stand it up without a multi-year migration

Our Agile Speed Framework is designed to deliver working capability in focused increments, so you see results against a real use case before committing to a full rollout.

Engagements typically begin with a single high-friction flow, such as payer remittance or patient collections, then expand across the revenue cycle. Because the platform is modular, you adopt the components you need and connect them to the systems you already run rather than replacing everything at once. ACM's ecosystem partners, Hanzo.ai and Lux Network, extend the platform with AI and settlement capabilities where deeper automation or on-chain rails add value.

The result is a healthcare finance stack that reduces administrative overhead, improves liquidity, and gives leadership a clearer, faster picture of money moving through the organization. Build it on ACM's core banking platform, then pair it with our broader payments and stablecoin settlement capabilities and your healthcare technology strategy to bring the full ecosystem under one roof.

Integration

Connects to the revenue-cycle systems you already run

A healthcare finance layer only earns its place if it works alongside your EHR, clearinghouse, and banking relationships. ACM connects through documented APIs and SDKs so finance and IT can wire settlement into existing workflows rather than rebuild them.

EHR, HIS & billing systems

Post payments and reconcile against patient accounts in your EHR or hospital information system, so accelerated settlement and automation reflect back into the source of truth your revenue-cycle staff already use.

Payer remittance & EDI

Ingest 835 remittance and 837 claim data from clearinghouses and payers, matching reimbursements to receivables automatically and routing only true exceptions to staff for review.

Banking, lockbox & KYC

Connect existing depository accounts, lockbox feeds, and card-network rails, and reuse the KYC/identity checks your institution has already established for patients and counterparties.

Administration & audit

Control and evidence finance and compliance teams can stand behind

Money moving through healthcare touches treasury, patient collections, and payer settlement at once, so who can do what, and proving it after the fact, matters as much as the rails themselves.

  • Role-based access: separate permissions for treasury, revenue-cycle, patient-collections, and read-only audit roles, enforcing segregation of duties across the flows the platform touches.
  • Single sign-on: federate access through your existing SSO and identity provider so staff use established credentials and offboarding stays centralized.
  • Immutable audit logs: every posting, settlement, and configuration change is recorded with actor and timestamp, designed to support SOC 2, HIPAA, and your internal control evidence.
  • Scoped administration: delegate configuration by entity or facility for multi-site health systems, without granting broad access across the organization.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is ACM's healthcare finance solution?

It is a regulated-first finance layer for hospitals, payers, and health systems that combines tokenized payment rails, revenue-cycle liquidity, treasury, patient payments, and administrative automation. It is white-label and client-owned, so the institution runs the ecosystem under its own brand.

How does it reduce administrative cost?

It automates reconciliation, posting, and exception handling, and moves settlement onto faster programmable rails. By shortening the path from service rendered to funds settled, it reduces the manual rework that drives much of healthcare's administrative spend, estimated at roughly $570B per year in the US by JAMA and Health Affairs.

Do we have to replace our existing systems?

No. The platform is modular and delivered through ACM's Agile Speed Framework. Most organizations start with one high-friction flow, such as payer remittance or patient collections, and connect ACM components to existing systems rather than undertaking a single large migration.

Is it secure and built for regulated institutions?

Yes. The platform is compliance-ready with audit trails and controls designed to support existing governance obligations, and it uses post-quantum cryptography to protect sensitive financial flows. Non-custodial settlement options are available where preferred.
