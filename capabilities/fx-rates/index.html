The Capability

Industry-leading exchange rates, owned by your institution

Most banks and credit unions rent FX from a third party and pass an opaque, marked-up rate to members and clients. ACM Global Tech builds the exchange layer into the platform you own and brand, so cross-border value moves at competitive rates with pricing your customers can actually see.

The result is a foreign-exchange experience that behaves like part of your core banking system because it is. Quotes, conversions, and settlement run on the same regulated-first infrastructure as deposits, payments, and treasury, with no separate vendor to integrate, reconcile, or explain to an examiner. Learn how it ships as a product in Exchange & FX.

Integration & Deployment

How the FX engine fits your existing core, ledger, and rails

For institutions that already run a core, a treasury workstation, and established KYC and payment vendors, the question is not whether the rates are competitive but how quote, conversion, and settlement events reach the systems your operations and finance teams already trust. ACM exposes the FX engine through documented APIs and SDKs so it slots into the infrastructure you have today.

Connectors to existing systems

REST and webhook APIs surface live quotes, lock-in rates, and conversion and settlement events to your core, general ledger, treasury workstation, and existing KYC and sanctions-screening providers, so multi-currency activity reconciles where you already book it.

Rate sourcing and routing

Aggregate pricing across liquidity sources, then govern spread, markup, and per-segment rate tiers through configuration rather than code, with every quote and the rate basis behind it captured for downstream pricing review.

Deployment and data residency

Run the exchange layer in cloud, hybrid, or single-tenant private deployments, with FX and settlement data held in the region your residency and cross-border-data obligations require.

Roles, SSO, and audit trails

Govern who can set spreads, approve rate overrides, or release cross-border settlement using role-based access, SSO into your identity provider, and immutable audit logs an examiner can trace quote-to-settlement.

  • No parallel reconciliation: because conversions post through connectors into your system of record, there is no second FX ledger for finance to true up at period close.
  • Phased rollout: stand up read-only rate display first, then enable conversion and cross-border settlement on the Agile Speed Framework as controls are signed off, with ecosystem support from Lux Network settlement infrastructure and the Hanzo.ai stack.
  • Post-quantum in transit and at rest: quote, conversion, and settlement traffic moving across these integrations is protected with post-quantum cryptography, designed to support SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI-DSS, and HIPAA-aligned requirements.

Frequently asked questions

What markets and currencies does ACM's FX capability cover?

ACM Global Tech delivers real-time multi-currency exchange and cross-border settlement across 200+ markets. Rates are built into your core banking stack, so quotes, conversions, and settlement run on the same infrastructure as deposits, payments, and treasury.

How are ACM's exchange rates more competitive than a third-party FX provider?

Because the exchange layer is part of the platform you own and brand, you control the spread instead of accepting an outside vendor's marked-up rate. Pricing is competitive and transparently displayed, so members and clients convert at the rate they see.

Is the FX capability white-label and client-owned?

Yes. Every screen, rate, and statement carries your brand, and the exchange capability is yours to price, position, and operate. It is delivered as part of ACM's complete white-label banking ecosystem.

Does ACM support stablecoin settlement for cross-border flows?

Yes. Eligible flows can settle over stablecoin rails for speed, with non-custodial options available where your operating model and risk appetite require them. All FX and settlement traffic is secured with post-quantum cryptography.
