Why it exists

Legacy delivery timelines are the real risk

Most core and platform programs stall because change is treated as a multi-year event instead of a continuous practice. The Agile Speed Framework is built to remove that drag.

Financial institutions do not lose ground because they lack ambition. They lose ground because every change to a legacy core is slow, expensive, and risky, so necessary work gets deferred. The Agile Speed Framework is ACM Global Tech's answer: a structured, repeatable way to design, ship, and prove banking technology in short cycles, without trading away the rigor a regulated institution requires.

The principle is simple. We deliver a working slice of the real system early, put it in front of the people who will run it, and iterate against measurable outcomes. You see progress in weeks, give feedback against something tangible, and keep control of scope, brand, and architecture the entire way.

The six stages

How the framework works

A clear path from first conversation to live system and measured results, with feedback built into every step.

1. Discovery

Co-collaboration and data-first analysis of your institution, your members or patients, and the systems already in place. We map outcomes before we map technology.

2. Strategy & Design

We co-create the roadmap and target architecture, sequenced around measurable results and the compliance posture your regulators and risk teams require.

3. Skeleton Deployment

A rapid, working prototype of the real platform stood up for instant speed to production, so stakeholders evaluate something live rather than a slide deck.

4. Feedback & Iteration

Real-time input from operators, executives, and end users drives continuous refinement. Direction changes are cheap because they happen early.

5. Full Deployment & Support

Production rollout across your footprint with ongoing optimization, hardening, and operational support as scale and usage grow.

6. Measurement

We track real outcomes against the goals set in Discovery: efficiency, security, and growth. The framework closes the loop instead of ending at go-live.

What makes it different

Speed that holds up to scrutiny

Fast delivery is only useful if the result is something a bank or credit union can actually run, defend, and own.

  • Skeleton deployments: we lead with a live, functional slice of the production system, so speed to production is real, not a prototype thrown away later.
  • Regulated-first by design: compliance, auditability, and controls are part of the build from stage one, not retrofitted before launch. The work is engineered to be examiner-ready and defensible.
  • Post-quantum security from day one: every solution is built with post-quantum cryptography, protecting long-lived financial records against harvest-now-decrypt-later exposure.
  • Client-owned and white-label: you brand and own what we deliver. The framework keeps architecture and intellectual property in your hands, never locked behind a vendor.
  • Flexible delivery models: onshore, nearshore, and offshore teams are matched to your timeline, budget, and risk tolerance.
  • Built for cost efficiency: the platform targets up to 95% lower infrastructure cost versus legacy core stacks, and the framework is how that value gets delivered without a disruptive rip-and-replace.
Where it applies

One method across the ecosystem

The framework is how every ACM Global Tech engagement is delivered, from a single capability to a complete platform.

Whether the work is modernizing a core banking platform, standing up deposits and lending, launching payments and mobile wallets, enabling exchange and multi-currency FX, or building tokenization and stablecoin settlement rails, the delivery approach is the same. That consistency is the point. Your teams learn one way of working with us, and they can extend it as the relationship grows. Institutions such as credit unions use it to modernize without a rip-and-replace; to see the framework applied to the most common starting point, explore our core & cloud modernization work, or talk to us about the capability you need first.

Rollout mechanics

How a skeleton deployment lands in your environment

The framework is engineered so each stage produces something a CIO and risk team can run against existing systems, not a sandbox that has to be rebuilt for production.

Connects to what you already run

Skeleton Deployment stands up against your existing core, ledger, KYC, and card-network connections through APIs and SDKs, so the early slice exchanges real data instead of mocks and proves integration before Full Deployment.

Scoped to one capability first

Each cycle targets a single capability, deposits, a mobile wallet, or stablecoin settlement, so rollout proceeds capability by capability and a stalled workstream never blocks the rest of the program.

Parallel-run, not rip-and-replace

New rails run alongside the legacy core during Feedback & Iteration, letting operators reconcile output and cut over on evidence rather than a single high-risk switch.

Your hosting model, from stage one

The skeleton is deployed into the cloud, hybrid, or single-tenant model you will run in production, so data-residency and isolation decisions are validated early, not renegotiated at go-live.

Governance & auditability

Each stage produces evidence, not just velocity

Because the framework is regulated-first, the artifacts your examiners, CISO, and procurement team need are outputs of the delivery cycle rather than a separate scramble before launch.

  • Discovery and Strategy & Design: produce the documented architecture, control mapping, and data-flow diagrams that evidence design intent against SOC 2, ISO 27001, PCI-DSS, and HIPAA requirements.
  • Change control across iterations: every iteration is versioned with a defined scope and approval gate, so accelerated delivery stays traceable and auditable rather than ad hoc.
  • Roles and access from the first slice: RBAC, SSO, and audit logging are configured in the skeleton, so administration and segregation of duties are tested early instead of retrofitted.
  • Measurement closes the loop: the final stage reports outcomes against the goals set in Discovery, giving leadership a defensible record of what changed and why.
  • One owned, documented platform: what ships is white-label and client-owned, with the architecture and runbooks handed to your teams, never locked behind a vendor.

This is how the Agile Speed Framework reconciles speed with scrutiny: the same cadence that gets working software in front of operators in weeks is the cadence that keeps the program examiner-ready end to end.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the Agile Speed Framework?

It is ACM Global Tech's delivery method for building and shipping regulated banking technology in short, iterative cycles. It moves through six stages, Discovery, Strategy & Design, Skeleton Deployment, Feedback & Iteration, Full Deployment & Support, and Measurement, so institutions see working software early and keep control of scope, brand, and architecture throughout.

What is a skeleton deployment?

A skeleton deployment is a rapid, working slice of the real production platform, stood up early so your operators and executives can evaluate something live instead of a slide deck. It delivers instant speed to production and makes feedback and course-correction inexpensive because they happen before full rollout.

How does fast delivery stay compliant?

Compliance, auditability, and controls are built in from the first stage rather than retrofitted before launch, and every solution ships with post-quantum cryptography. The framework is regulated-first and engineered so the work is examiner-ready and defensible, which is why speed does not come at the expense of rigor.

Do we own what ACM builds?

Yes. Everything delivered through the Agile Speed Framework is white-label and client-owned. You brand and own the platform and its architecture, and ACM Global Tech offers onshore, nearshore, and offshore delivery models matched to your timeline, budget, and risk tolerance.
