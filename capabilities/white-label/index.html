What ownership means

Client-owned, not vendor-rented

Most banking technology asks you to operate inside someone else's product. ACM inverts that model. The brand, the data, the customer relationship, and the roadmap belong to your institution.

  • Your brand, everywhere: apps, statements, cards, and web flows carry your name and visual identity — not ours.
  • Your data, your rules: account, transaction, and member data stay governed by your institution under clear data-ownership terms.
  • Your customer relationship: members bank with you. ACM stays invisible behind the experience.
  • Your roadmap: configure products, fees, and workflows without waiting in a shared vendor backlog.
One brandable ecosystem

Everything under one identity

Ownership is only useful if it spans the whole stack. Each capability below ships white-label and brandable, so your institution presents a single, coherent product instead of a patchwork of vendor screens.

Core banking

Deposits, lending, and account servicing on a modern core — configured and branded as your own.

Payments & wallets

Exchange & multi-currency FX

RWA tokenization

Stablecoin settlement

Programmable settlement rails that move value behind the scenes while members see only your brand.

Card issuing

Treasury & liquidity

Mobile & web

Member-facing apps and portals themed to your identity, down to color, copy, and component.

Built for regulated institutions

Regulated-first by design

White-label should never mean cutting corners on controls. ACM is built compliance-ready for credit unions, community and mid-sized banks, and healthcare organizations that answer to serious oversight.

  • Compliance-ready foundations: audit trails, role-based access, and configurable controls that fit the way regulated institutions actually operate.
  • Post-quantum cryptography: forward-looking encryption protects member data and settlement well past today's standards.
  • Non-custodial options: for tokenized assets and digital-currency flows, your institution can retain custody and control rather than hand it to a third party.
  • Clear ownership terms: brand, data, and configuration rights are written into the relationship, not assumed.
How we deliver

Branded and live, faster

Ownership and speed are not at odds. ACM's Agile Speed Framework stands up a fully branded environment without the multi-year integration cycles legacy cores demand, with ecosystem support from Hanzo.ai and Lux Financial.

Your team configures products, applies your identity, and connects the capabilities you need — while ACM, Hanzo.ai, and Lux Financial handle the underlying engineering, AI and data tooling, and cryptography. The result is a platform that looks and behaves like it was built in-house, because in every way that matters to your members, it is yours.

Fintechs and embedded-finance teams use the same model to launch branded products quickly without owning a banking core from scratch. Explore that path on our fintech solutions page.

Owning the integrations

Your brand on top, your existing rails underneath

Owning the experience does not mean ripping out what works. ACM's white-label stack connects to the systems your institution already runs, so the customer sees one brand while your back office keeps the connections it depends on.

Core & ledger connectors

Run the ACM core as system of record or bridge to an incumbent core and general ledger, so branded front ends and your books stay reconciled during and after migration.

KYC, AML & card networks

Wire your chosen KYC/AML, sanctions-screening, and card-network providers into branded onboarding and issuing flows — your vendors, your contracts, your name on the screen.

Open APIs & SDKs

REST and webhook APIs plus mobile and web SDKs let your developers extend the branded apps, embed flows in existing channels, and keep building on a stack you control.

Administration & exit rights

Ownership you can administer and walk away with

White-label ownership only counts if your team can govern the platform day to day and leave on your own terms. ACM writes operational control and portability into the relationship, not just the brand on the login screen.

  • Role-based access & SSO: map product, treasury, compliance, and support duties to your own roles and identity provider, so administration mirrors your org chart rather than a vendor's.
  • Immutable audit logs: every configuration change, fee adjustment, and privileged action is recorded for examiners and internal review, designed to support SOC 2 and ISO 27001 evidence requirements.
  • Configuration in your hands: products, fees, branding, and workflows are yours to change without filing a vendor ticket or joining a shared backlog.
  • Defined exit & data portability: documented data-export and configuration-handover terms mean your member, account, and transaction data leaves with you — no hostage data, no rip-out penalty.

The test of ownership is whether you could change vendors and keep your brand, your data, and your customer relationships intact. The white-label model is built so the answer is yes.

Frequently asked questions

What does white-label banking ownership actually include?

It means your institution owns and brands the experience end to end. Apps, statements, cards, and portals carry your identity; member data is governed under your institution's terms; and you control product configuration, fees, and roadmap. ACM provides the underlying core, payments, FX, tokenization, and card-issuing technology while staying invisible to your members.

Who owns the customer data on an ACM platform?

Your institution does. ACM operates on a client-owned model with clear data-ownership terms, so account, transaction, and member data stay governed by your institution rather than locked inside a vendor's product.

Is the platform suitable for regulated banks and credit unions?

Yes. ACM is regulated-first and compliance-ready, with audit trails, role-based access, configurable controls, post-quantum cryptography, and non-custodial options for tokenized and digital-currency flows. It is built for credit unions, community and mid-sized banks, and healthcare organizations under serious oversight.

How quickly can a branded environment go live?
