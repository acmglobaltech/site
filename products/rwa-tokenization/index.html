Overview

From legacy ledgers to programmable assets

Tokenization moves an asset's record of ownership onto programmable infrastructure, where transfer, settlement, and reporting happen as one step instead of three.

For a regulated institution, the value is operational before it is speculative. A tokenized instrument carries its own compliance logic, who can hold it, under what conditions, and how it settles, so reconciliation, transfer-agent work, and end-of-day matching collapse into the asset itself. That means faster settlement, fewer intermediaries, and a cleaner audit trail.

ACM delivers this as a complete, client-owned system. You define the asset classes, the eligibility rules, and the brand. We provide the issuance engine, the settlement rails, and the registry underneath, engineered to drive total cost of ownership down, with post-quantum cryptography protecting long-lived records from day one. It runs alongside the same core banking platform that powers deposits, lending, and payments.

How it works

From asset to settled token

A clear, four-step lifecycle your team controls end to end.

1. Define

Set the asset class, eligibility rules, and disclosures. Your governance becomes the token's built-in logic.

2. Issue

Mint the asset on ACM's issuance engine, branded as your own, with holders recorded in the registry.

3. Settle

Transfer and settle against stablecoins or fiat rails, with finality and reporting captured in one step.

4. Register

Maintain a continuous, auditable record of ownership ready for examiners, auditors, and counterparties.

Integration & Interoperability

Plugs into the systems of record you already run

Tokenization doesn't replace your core, your transfer-agent function, or your KYC stack, ACM's issuance engine, settlement rails, and registry connect to them through documented APIs so the token becomes one more synchronized view of an asset you already control.

Core & ledger sync

REST and event APIs reconcile every mint, transfer, and redemption back to your general ledger and the ACM banking core, so on-chain positions and book-of-record balances never drift apart.

KYC, custody & chain connectors

Wire eligibility checks to your existing KYC/AML and sanctions providers, route holdings to your chosen custodian or qualified-custody arrangement, and issue across the chains your governance approves, no rip-and-replace.

Registry as source of truth

The holder-and-transfer registry exposes read APIs and webhooks for downstream reporting, transfer-agent workflows, and counterparty confirmations, with stablecoin and FX settlement interoperating through ACM's exchange.

  • Issuance under control: role-based permissions gate who can define an asset class, approve a mint, freeze a holding, or sign off on a redemption, mapped to your SSO and segregation-of-duties policy.
  • Tamper-evident audit trail: every eligibility decision, transfer restriction, and registry update is logged immutably and exportable for examiners, internal audit, and counterparties.
  • Built to support your obligations: connectors and controls are engineered to support SOC 2, ISO 27001, and the reporting requirements your regulators and auditors already hold you to.

The result is a tokenization layer that extends your existing architecture rather than forking it, so adoption is an integration project on the Agile Speed Framework, not a migration off the systems your institution depends on.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is RWA tokenization?

Real-world asset (RWA) tokenization moves an asset's record of ownership onto programmable infrastructure, so transfer, settlement, and reporting happen as a single auditable action. For regulated institutions the immediate benefit is operational: faster settlement, fewer intermediaries, and a cleaner audit trail. ACM provides the issuance engine, settlement rails, and registry as a client-owned, white-label platform.

Is the platform built for regulated institutions?

Yes. ACM is regulated-first and compliance-ready by design. Eligibility, transfer restrictions, and reporting are enforced in the asset logic itself, and a built-in registry gives you a single source of truth for examiners and auditors. We do not make representations about specific licenses or charters; the platform is engineered to support your institution's own regulatory obligations.

What kinds of assets can be tokenized?

The platform supports tokenized treasuries, deposit and fund instruments, private credit and loans, healthcare receivables, and stablecoin settlement, all on one issuance and settlement layer. You define the asset classes and eligibility rules; ACM provides the rails and registry underneath.

How does stablecoin settlement work with tokenized assets?

Tokenized assets can settle against stablecoins for near-instant, 24/7 finality, with non-custodial options available where your governance requires them. Settlement and reporting are captured in one step, and the rails interoperate with ACM's multi-currency exchange so FX and settlement move together.
